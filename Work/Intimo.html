Concept Statement

Intimo is an online radio experience that employs user-generated sound bites to help people connect. Private listening on headphones is a way to experience intimacy during mandated social distancing. Our interface gives users a way to share snippets of audio while socially distant.

The Team

  • Esther Nam - Project manager, UX/UI Designer
  • Dar Min - Sound designer, Creative technologist
  • Myself - Tech researcher and developer

Why We Made This

Community

The pandemic has separated person from person, but has brought us a shared experience. We are all living through this moment. We wanted to create an outlet through which people could share their quarantine memories or moments, and listen to what others have to say--allowing for people to relate, be comforted by, or learn from the experiences of others.

Intimacy

As we start to miss physical and emotional interaction, our goal was to reimagine a new way to experience intimacy without touch. By creating visual and audible experiences, we hope that we can allow people to ‘be’ with others in a different way.

Nostalgia

Our society is very digitally connected, and we are used to getting what we want from the apps and programs we use in a matter of seconds. We wanted to bring back the idea of waiting to hear what you or others have shared. Mimicking the idea of radio events is something we thought would create a new experience for the user, and give them a break from the immediacy of social media.

Key Features

Scheduled Radio

Feeling nostalgic? Eagerly wait for your audio to be played at a specific time on the Intimo radio as it will begin playing at a set time on a specific day.

Generative Background

The background is generated based on the weather of the zipcode the user chooses to input, allowing them to see and ‘feel’ the environment of another.

Built-In Recorder

No need to use a third-party recording function. Our platform has a built in recording service to let you record in real time.

Archive

Explore through the Intimo sound archive and listen to a short glimpse of how others are spending their quarantine.
